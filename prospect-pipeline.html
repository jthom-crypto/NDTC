Credit Unions

Federally chartered credit unions are not currently authorized to custody cryptocurrencies or other digital assets directly, while state-chartered authority depends on state law. For many credit unions, a regulated third-party custody partnership may be the practical path to member-facing digital asset services.

InstitutionSizingSignal
St. Cloud Financial Credit Union
St. Cloud, MN
 ~20% of members hold crypto Launched CU-Digital Asset Vault, a hybrid self-custody model that keeps members in control while connecting digital asset activity to a trusted credit union relationship, and secured a Lloyd's-backed insurance partnership for its custody operations. Strong signal of member demand, though not the same as full-service custodial authority.
Randolph-Brooks Federal Credit Union
Live Oak, TX
 $25B combined pilot cohort Founding participant in the Stablecore, Circuit, and Curql digital asset pilot; actively testing stablecoin and crypto infrastructure now.
Stanford Federal Credit Union
Palo Alto, CA
 Pilot cohort member Same Stablecore/Circuit/Curql pilot; a tech-forward membership base likely to push for faster rollout than peer institutions.
La Capitol Federal Credit Union
Baton Rouge, LA
 Pilot cohort member Third founding member of the same pilot. A cluster of three credit unions already moving in parallel is an efficient entry point for one coordinated NDTC pitch.
UNIFY Financial Credit Union
Torrance, CA
 ~$3B assets Early Q2/NYDIG bitcoin launch partner among the first credit unions to test member-facing bitcoin access.
Curql Collective (CUSO)
160+ member credit unions
 160+ CU network A credit union service organization funding fintech pilots across its network, and an early investor in Stablecore. The highest-leverage single relationship in this segment if NDTC becomes its preferred custody partner.

Broker-Dealers & RIAs

RIAs with custody of client funds, securities, or crypto assets face SEC custody expectations that call for qualified custody workflows. Banks, broker-dealers, and certain trust-company structures are central to that conversation.

Sub-national footprint

Corporate Treasuries

Public companies and digital asset treasury vehicles holding BTC need institutional custody, governance controls, audit-ready reporting, and treasury workflow support. The best prospects are large enough to need institutional infrastructure, but still early enough to be evaluating custody, financing, and reporting partners.

InstitutionSizingSignal
Twenty One Capital (XXI)
Tether-backed
 ~43,500 BTC at launch Bitcoin-native treasury company that listed on the NYSE in December 2025 with over 43,500 BTC; Anchorage is the disclosed custodian per its 10-K. Tether has since acquired SoftBank's stake. Best framed as a financing, reporting, or treasury-workflow conversation rather than a base custody gap.
Metaplanet Inc.
Tokyo-listed
 ~43,000 BTC ($2.6B-$2.7B) Non-US issuer with the world's third-largest corporate BTC treasury as of July 2026, still accumulating toward a stated 100,000 BTC year-end target. Would need a US-regulated custody counterparty for any USD-denominated financing against the position.
Bitcoin Standard Treasury Company (BSTR)
Adam Back / Blockstream
 30,021 BTC planned Planned Nasdaq listing via SPAC merger with 30,021 BTC; as of July 8, 2026 the original merger terms were scrapped and a new deal is being renegotiated. Custody and financing infrastructure remain unsettled, a genuine greenfield opportunity while the structure is still in motion.
Non-crypto-native operating companies adding BTC
Diversified small/mid-cap issuers
 Emerging cohort A growing group of operating businesses outside crypto and mining are adding BTC as a treasury diversification line, typically with the least internal expertise and the highest need for a turnkey fiduciary custodian.
MARA Holdings
Bitcoin mining
 ~36,300 BTC (verify before outreach) Large public Bitcoin miner and corporate BTC holder. Holdings have moved substantially after disclosed sales, from a peak near 53,800 BTC at the end of 2025 to roughly 36,300 BTC by mid-June 2026, so current position should be reconfirmed directly before any outreach.
