Section 01

The charter race, at a glance

The market is still being formed. Several major crypto and fintech firms are pursuing the same OCC national trust bank path, but most are still in conditional approval or pre-opening stages. For prospects, the question is no longer only "why a trust bank?" It is "which custody model fits our institution best?"

2021
Anchorage Digital Bank becomes the first operating federal trust-bank precedent for institutional digital assets, with years of OCC supervision and examination history.
Dec 9, 2025
OCC Interpretive Letter 1188 confirms national banks may engage in riskless principal crypto-asset transactions as part of the business of banking.
Dec 12, 2025
OCC conditionally approves five national trust bank charter applications in a single day: two de novo entities (Circle's First National Digital Currency Bank, Ripple National Trust Bank) and three conversions from existing state trust charters (BitGo Bank & Trust, Fidelity Digital Assets, Paxos Trust Company).
Feb 2026
Bridge National Trust Bank and National Digital Trust Company receive preliminary conditional OCC approval.
Feb 23, 2026
Foris DAX National Trust Bank, a Crypto.com affiliate, receives preliminary conditional OCC approval.
Mar 2026
OCC finalizes a rule clarifying that national trust banks may conduct non-fiduciary activities in addition to fiduciary activities.
Apr 2026
Coinbase receives OCC approval to charter a national trust bank, migrating custody from its existing New York-chartered Coinbase Custody Trust Company over a three-year transition, and facilitating custody clients' access to affiliate products including Staking, Prime Trading, and Prime Financing. The model is useful as a comparison, but Coinbase's natural customer base is different from NDTC's.
Jul 10, 2026
Circle's First National Digital Currency Bank (Circle National Trust) becomes the first entity in this new wave to receive final OCC approval, moving from conditional approval to authorization. Every other 2025-2026 entrant, including NDTC, remains in the conditional-approval or pre-opening stage.
Section 02

Where NDTC sits in this field

NDTC's charter application has continuity with Protego Trust's earlier submission. Protego received conditional OCC approval in 2021 before the approval expired. Protego Holdings later refiled under National Digital Trust Company, which received conditional approval in February 2026.

NDTC does not need to compete on brand scale with Coinbase, Fidelity, BitGo, Paxos, Circle, or Ripple. Its opening is focus: banks, credit unions, broker-dealers, corporate treasuries, and fintechs that want regulated digital asset infrastructure without building it themselves.

Section 03

Battlecard: the field

Charter status, what each player actually offers today, and the honest angle for a conversation where a prospect brings up the name.

Anchorage Digital Bank, N.A.
Operating benchmark
Operational since 2021
What they offer
Institutional digital asset custody, staking, trading, governance, and settlement services, anchored by Anchorage Digital Bank's national trust charter. HSM-based key architecture. Backed by a16z, GIC, Goldman Sachs, KKR, and Visa at a $4.2B valuation.
Where they're strong
Five years of operating history and OCC examination cycles that no other charter applicant, including NDTC, can yet point to. The reference case for "this model works."
NDTC angle
Anchorage validates the OCC trust-bank model for institutional digital assets. NDTC should not compete on tenure. It should focus on where it is a better fit: banks, credit unions, fintechs, and institutions looking for a dedicated custody partner.
Sources: Ledger Insights coverage of OCC 2021 approval; Anchorage Digital
Coinbase National Trust Bank
Large-scale custody ecosystem
Approved Apr 2026, migrating
What they offer
Migrating custody from Coinbase Custody Trust Company over a three-year de novo period. The bank would provide fiduciary digital asset custody and facilitate custody clients' access to certain Coinbase affiliate products, including Staking, Prime Trading, and Prime Financing, provided by affiliates rather than the bank itself.
Where they're strong
Large existing custody base, brand recognition, and an exchange ecosystem already wired into most crypto-native institutions.
NDTC angle
Coinbase is the closest product-ecosystem comparison, but its core audience is crypto-native and exchange-adjacent. NDTC can focus on banks, credit unions, and fintech partners that want a more dedicated custody relationship.
Sources: OCC Corporate Decision #1370 (Apr 2026)
BitGo Bank & Trust, N.A.
Converting from state charter, IPO-track
Conditionally approved Dec 2025
What they offer
Digital asset custody, fiduciary services, escrow services, and stablecoin-related activity, regulated by more than 50 U.S. and foreign regulators today. Filed for a U.S. IPO in 2025.
Where they're strong
Deep institutional and exchange relationships. Its IPO process also gives it visibility and potential access to capital.
NDTC angle
BitGo is strongest with crypto-native and capital markets clients. NDTC can focus on banks and credit unions asking a simpler question: who safely holds digital assets for our members?
Sources: OCC conditional approval decision (Dec 12, 2025); BitGo Holdings SEC Form S-1/A
Fidelity Digital Assets, N.A.
Converting from state charter, TradFi-backed
Conditionally approved Dec 2025
What they offer
Institutional custody and trade execution since 2018, now converting to a national trust charter. Backed by the full scale and balance sheet of Fidelity Investments. Launched Fidelity Digital Dollar (FIDD) in February 2026, a stablecoin issued by Fidelity Digital Assets, National Association.
Where they're strong
Strong brand trust with large asset managers, pension funds, and RIAs already inside the Fidelity ecosystem.
NDTC angle
Fidelity has major brand trust with institutions already in its ecosystem. NDTC can focus on clients looking for a dedicated digital asset custody partner, not an extension of a broader brokerage relationship.
Sources: OCC conditional approval announcement (Dec 12, 2025); Fidelity Investments press release (Feb 4, 2026)
Paxos Trust Company, N.A.
Converting from state charter, issuance-focused
Conditionally approved Dec 2025
What they offer
Custody of crypto assets, fiat, securities, and commodities, alongside U.S. dollar-backed and gold-backed digital asset issuance and exchange/brokerage/trade facilitation services, now converting its NY trust charter to a national one.
Where they're strong
Deep issuance and brokerage-as-a-service infrastructure for large consumer fintechs and exchanges.
NDTC angle
Paxos is strongest as issuance and infrastructure for large platforms. NDTC can focus on direct custody relationships with banks and credit unions.
Sources: OCC conditional approval announcement (Dec 12, 2025)
Circle National Trust
De novo, issuer-first
Final approval, Jul 10, 2026
What they offer
The first entity in this new wave to reach final OCC approval. Will initially provide fiduciary custody for Circle's own USDC reserve and affiliates, with a stated plan to potentially extend custody to banks and regulated derivatives organizations later, depending on demand.
Where they're strong
Direct federal oversight of reserves backing one of the largest regulated dollar stablecoins. Circle is also the furthest along of the 2025-2026 charter wave.
NDTC angle
Circle National Trust is initially focused on Circle's own reserve and affiliate needs. Broader third-party custody may come later, but it is not the first use case.
Sources: Circle press release, BusinessWire (Jul 10, 2026)
Ripple National Trust Bank
De novo, issuer-first
Conditionally approved Dec 2025
What they offer
Plans to manage a segregated reserve of liquid assets underlying RLUSD on behalf of its affiliate Standard Custody & Trust, and to perform collateral trustee services for RLUSD holders on a fiduciary basis, alongside cryptocurrency custody for affiliates and unaffiliated institutional clients.
Where they're strong
Dual state and federal oversight positioning for RLUSD, and an existing enterprise payments relationship base through Ripple.
NDTC angle
Ripple National Trust Bank is built first around supporting Ripple's own stablecoin infrastructure. Unaffiliated institutional custody is a secondary offering, not the bank's core purpose.
Sources: OCC Conditional Approval #1359 (Dec 12, 2025)
Fireblocks / Fireblocks Trust Company
Wallet infrastructure plus NYDFS-regulated custody
NYDFS-chartered (state, not OCC)
What they offer
MPC-based wallet infrastructure and a settlement network used by 2,400+ organizations and 80+ banks, including BNY Mellon and Revolut. Fireblocks Trust Company, a separate limited-purpose trust company chartered by NYDFS, additionally offers qualified digital asset custody, segregated and bankruptcy-remote, to clients including Bakkt, FalconX, and Galaxy.
Where they're strong
The dominant technology layer for institutions that want to self-custody, now paired with a state-regulated custody option for clients who want both under one platform.
NDTC angle
Fireblocks combines wallet infrastructure with NYDFS-regulated custody. NDTC's differentiation is the OCC national trust bank path and a custody-first model for institutions that specifically want federal supervision.
Sources: Fireblocks Trust Company product page; TradingView/Cointelegraph (Aug 2024)

Section 05

Objection handling

Quick responses to the questions most likely to come up in a first conversation.

"Why would we go with a company still 'In Organization' instead of an established name?"
Most of the market is still in buildout mode. Anchorage is the operating benchmark, and Circle has reached final approval, but many major names are still moving through conditional approval, conversion, or launch requirements. NDTC's "In Organization" status is not unusual in this market.
"Isn't Fireblocks (or a similar MPC provider) enough on its own?"
Fireblocks is a strong wallet and custody platform, but it is not the same model as an OCC national trust bank. The question is not whether Fireblocks is useful. It is whether the institution specifically wants an OCC-supervised custody partner.
"Why not just wait for a bigger name like Coinbase or Fidelity to build this for us?"
Coinbase and Fidelity are strong options, but their natural channels are different. NDTC can focus on banks, credit unions, fintechs, and corporate treasuries that want a dedicated custody partner rather than a broader exchange or asset-management platform.
"What can a national trust charter actually do that a state charter can't?"
A national trust charter gives institutions a single federal supervisory framework. State-chartered custodians can still be viable, but an OCC-supervised model may be cleaner for institutions operating across markets or serving clients in multiple states.
